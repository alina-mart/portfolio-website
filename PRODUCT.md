# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Game studio recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Unity gameplay scripters. They land on the portfolio with limited time, scanning for proof of skill, shipped work, and a sense of who Alina is beyond a CV bullet list. They need to feel confident she can contribute to a real production and that she will be pleasant to work with.

## Product Purpose

Alina Martynchik's portfolio exists to convert a recruiter's first impression into a contact or interview. Success means a hiring manager walks away knowing: she has shipped Unity 2D games (not just tutorials), she comes from a real production background (5+ years in game localization and pipeline automation), and she has the aesthetic curiosity to care about craft. The site is also her professional first impression — it must match the personality and attention to detail she claims.

## Brand Personality

Darkly playful, technically earnest, quietly confident. Think: an RPG character sheet that takes itself seriously but still winks at you. The d20 dice is the brand metaphor — chance, skill, game culture, all in one object.

## Anti-references

- **Bland dev CV sites**: generic GitHub Pages defaults, flat color, no personality, wall-of-text projects
- **Neon-heavy cyberpunk**: hot pink / cyan synthwave overdrive — too genre-specific, too loud
- **Minimalist / white-space portfolios**: clean light-mode "design portfolio" aesthetics — wrong register for a game scripter
- **Horror / grotesque**: unsettling dark aesthetics — the portfolio should feel empowering and capable, not threatening

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over claims** — show shipped games, real screenshots, real flavor text; don't describe skills in the abstract when a project card can demonstrate them.
2. **Game culture, not cosplay** — the gothic-fantasy tone earns its place because games are genuinely Alina's world; every aesthetic choice should feel motivated, not decorative.
3. **Confident legibility** — dark palette and display type should feel authoritative, not impenetrable; body text is always readable, hierarchy is always clear.
4. **Interactive intentionality** — the d20 is the hero interaction; every other motion or hover state should feel purposeful, not reflexive.
5. **First impression = job application** — the portfolio competes with other candidates; every rough edge (contrast failure, overflow, broken layout on tablet) is a missed hire.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum for contrast. `prefers-reduced-motion` already implemented — maintain it in all future changes. Semantic HTML landmarks for screen-reader navigation. All interactive elements need visible focus states.
