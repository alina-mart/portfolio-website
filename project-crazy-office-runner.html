Project Type

2D endless runner built for Yandex Games (WebGL + mobile)

An independent personal project developed in a team of two. Core concept developed collaboratively. Unity development and gameplay systems by me; UI/UX, art, and visual direction by my co-creator. Game design was a shared effort. Spine animation outsourced.

Currently in late development: core gameplay complete, pending Yandex platform integration, leaderboard implementation, obstacles animation, and fine-tuning of art assets.

Creative Context

The game follows an office worker who, fed up with endless tasks and revisions, snaps, jumps on an office chair and rides through the chaos she has unleashed. The player jumps and ducks to avoid colleagues and flying obstacles across 5 distinct office environments, with speed and enemy complexity increasing over time.

Features full animation and sound design, score-based progression, and a final score tied to distance.

My Contribution

  • Handled the full Unity side of the project: gameplay systems, scene setup, UI integration, and build preparation for WebGL and mobile.
  • Built the core runner systems from scratch including player controller, jump mechanics, obstacle spawning, scoring, game-over flow, and restart states, making sure everything held up across platforms.
  • Implemented varied obstacle patterns to keep the challenge feeling fresh as the game progresses.
  • Worked on responsive UI, input handling, WebGL and mobile compatibility, and kept the build size in check.
  • Added audio feedback throughout the project, including player actions, game over, and score milestones.
  • Was the main technical point of contact for the UI/UX artist: figured out feature questions together, unblocked things when they got stuck, and turned visual ideas into working implementations.
  • Coordinated with the outsourced Spine animator on what was needed, gave feedback through several revision rounds, and handled the full integration into Unity.
